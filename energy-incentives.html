What can you save? VAT & incentives in the UK and Ireland

Below is a simple overview of how VAT and some government schemes can help with the cost of a home battery or solar setup. Rules and amounts can change, and your situation may differ—so we always recommend checking the official links for the latest information.

United Kingdom

You can benefit from 0% VAT on battery storage and, if you have solar, payments for exporting energy to the grid.

What you can save or benefit from

  • 0% VAT on home battery systems — You don’t pay VAT on the supply and installation of a battery storage system. That can mean a noticeable saving compared to the standard VAT rate (e.g. on a typical system this could be in the hundreds or more—exact amount depends on your installer and system).
  • Smart Export Guarantee (SEG) — If you have solar panels and export unused electricity to the grid, you can get paid for it. How much depends on the tariff you’re on. Details: SEG on Ofgem’s website.
  • Other schemes — Depending on where you live, there may be local or national loans or grants. It’s worth checking gov.uk or your local authority for current schemes.

Conditions to be aware of

  • 0% VAT applies to qualifying battery storage installations in the UK; your installer can confirm eligibility.
  • SEG is for exporting solar electricity—you need solar panels and a compatible setup.

Ireland

You can benefit from 0% VAT when batteries are installed with solar, and from a grant towards solar panels (no separate grant for batteries alone).

What you can save or benefit from

  • 0% VAT on batteries when installed with solar — If you install a battery together with solar panels, the battery (and qualifying work) can be zero-rated for VAT, which reduces the overall cost.
  • Solar panel grant — You may be able to get a grant of up to €1,800 towards solar panel installation. There is no separate grant for a battery on its own. Full details and eligibility: SEAI Solar Electricity Grant.

Conditions to be aware of

  • In Ireland, the 0% VAT treatment for batteries applies when they are installed with solar panels. If you install a battery only (no solar), different VAT rules may apply—check with your installer or revenue authority.
  • The solar grant is subject to SEAI eligibility criteria and application process.

Quick comparison

  • UK: 0% VAT on home battery systems (standalone or with solar). SEG payments if you export solar to the grid. No dedicated grant for batteries alone.
  • Ireland: 0% VAT on batteries when installed together with solar; grant of up to €1,800 for solar (no separate battery grant).
  • In both countries, incentives and eligibility can change. Always confirm with official sources or your installer before making decisions.
